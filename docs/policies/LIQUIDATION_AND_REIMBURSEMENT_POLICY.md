# IOCT Liquidation & Reimbursement Policy

## 1. Core Philosophy
Quarterly or bi-annual reimbursement is an anti-pattern. It effectively turns employees into zero-interest lenders to the company, eroding trust and morale. IOCT will reimburse employees promptly on a predictable, short cycle tied to the standard Philippine semi-monthly payroll.

## 2. Terminology
- **Cash Advance (CA):** Company money released *before* spend (e.g., project fund, field travel).
- **Liquidation:** Employee accounting for a cash advance with valid receipts, returning any unused amount.
- **Reimbursement:** Employee spent their *own* money for an authorized expense; the company pays them back.

## 3. Disbursement Cadence (The Standard)
Reimbursements will be processed alongside the standard semi-monthly payroll (15th and end-of-month).
- **Submission Cutoff:** 3 working days before each payroll date.
- **Payment SLA:** Complete claims approved before the cutoff are paid on that payroll date.
- **Maximum SLA:** Reimbursements must be paid no later than 15 calendar days from the date of a *complete* submission.

## 4. Submission and Approval Requirements
- **Timeliness:** Expenses must be submitted within 5-10 working days of being incurred.
- **Completeness:** A submission is only "filed" when it includes:
  - Date, amount, and business purpose/project.
  - Valid BIR-compliant receipts (Official Receipt for services, Sales Invoice for goods).
  - Supplier TIN and details for purchases over ₱1,000.
  - Incomplete submissions will be returned within 2 working days and do not start the SLA clock.
- **Approval:** Managers must review within 2-3 business days.

## 5. Cash Advances & Liquidations
- **Window:** Cash advances must be liquidated within 5-10 working days of the activity's completion.
- **Rule:** "No liquidation, no new CA." A new advance will not be released if a prior one is unliquidated.
- **Unliquidated Balances:** May be subject to payroll deduction, but only following proper written notice and DOLE guidelines regarding authorized deductions.

## 6. Corporate Cards & Petty Cash (Alternative Options)
- **Petty Cash Fund (PCF):** An office petty cash fund (e.g., ₱5,000) for same-day small purchases under ₱1,000.
- **Corporate/Virtual Cards:** For recurring SaaS, ads, or large purchases, company cards should be used to avoid employees fronting significant capital.

## 7. DOLE & BIR Compliance
- Properly liquidated expenses with official receipts are non-taxable.
- Deductions from wages for unliquidated amounts will strictly follow DOLE authorized deduction rules.
